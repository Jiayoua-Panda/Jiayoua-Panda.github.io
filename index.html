About

Background

I'm a vehicle engineer with a multidisciplinary background spanning aerospace manufacturing, software engineering, and automotive safety research. My undergraduate training at Nanjing University of Aeronautics and Astronautics grounded me in control systems and precision engineering, before I moved into production engineering at AVIC Chengdu Aircraft — where I contributed to manufacturing process systems and earned my first patent as lead inventor.

Several years in the gaming industry sharpened my software engineering instincts: shipping real-time systems under strict performance constraints in C++, driving game engine optimisation, and owning full feature pipelines across multiple live-service titles. These skills translate directly to the latency-critical software stacks of autonomous driving.

At Chalmers University of Technology I've focused on vehicle safety and dynamics — analysing crash biomechanics, active safety maneuvers, ride comfort on electric buses, and developing simulation-based driver assistance for articulated trucks. I'm looking to bring this combination of engineering rigour and software fluency to companies building the next generation of freight and mobility.

Vehicle Dynamics · ADAS

Reversing Aid for Articulated Trucks

Model-based reversing assistance system for semi-trailer trucks, built in MATLAB/Simulink. Kinematically models the articulated joint geometry to compute real-time steering guidance for precise low-speed manoeuvring in constrained environments such as loading docks and terminals — directly relevant to autonomous freight operations. Validated across multiple trailer configurations via simulation.

Ride Comfort Analysis — Volvo 7900 Electric Bus

Signal processing and statistical analysis on vibration data from Volvo 7900 Electric City Buses, benchmarked against ISO 2631. Quantified how the electric powertrain's torque delivery profile alters cabin vibration versus combustion counterparts.

Bicycle Overtaking — Active Safety

Studied driver behaviour and vehicle dynamics during bicycle overtaking to establish safe lateral clearance requirements and collision risk thresholds across varying speeds and road conditions. Findings directly inform ADAS intervention trigger logic for urban mixed-traffic scenarios.

Crash Pulse & Restraint Systems — Passive Safety

Relationship between vehicle crash pulse characteristics and occupant injury risk. Analysed how seatbelt force limiters, airbag timing, and crash severity interact to determine injury outcomes.
